What is an Aurora Borealis?

The Aurora Borealis is a natural phenomenon that is caused by a solar storm from the sun.

Sometimes, the sun will let out a burst of gas that goes around the universe.

When it reaches Earth, it reacts with Earth's ozone layer and reacts with all the molecules in there,

this creates all the cool colors that you may see in the sky!

Do you know what colors are in this phenomenon?

There are:

  • greenish-yellow
  • blue
  • and red!

Other Facts about the Aurora Borealis!

  1. The Aurora Borealis are also called the "Northern Lights"
  2. The lights are constantly moving
  3. Different colors mean different reactions!

Different Reastions? What are these Reactions?

Different colors in the Aurora Borealis mean different things, like:

  • Greenish-yellow - Monatomic oxygen
  • Blue - Ionized diatomic nitrogen
  • Red - Monatomic oxygen, Diatomic nitrogen
